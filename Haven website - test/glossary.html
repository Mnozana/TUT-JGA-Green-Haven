Gardening Glossary

Key terms every gardener should know

No matching terms found. Try a different search.

Annual

A plant that completes its life cycle in one growing season, from seed to flower to seed.

Biennial

A plant that takes two years to complete its life cycle, typically growing leaves the first year and flowering the second.

Compost

Decomposed organic matter used to enrich soil, improve its structure, and provide nutrients to plants.

Deadheading

The practice of removing spent flowers to encourage more blooms and prevent seed production.

Hardiness Zone

A geographic area defined by its climate conditions, especially minimum temperatures, that determines which plants are likely to thrive there.

Mulch

A protective layer of material (such as wood chips, straw, or leaves) spread on top of soil to conserve moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate temperature.

Perennial

A plant that lives for more than two years, typically flowering and setting seed each year.

pH

A measure of soil acidity or alkalinity on a scale from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most alkaline), with 7 being neutral.

Pruning

The practice of cutting back plants to control growth, remove dead or diseased material, or encourage flowering and fruiting.

Transplanting

Moving a plant from one location to another, either within a garden or from a container to the ground.

Germination

The process by which a plant grows from a seed, beginning when the seed absorbs water and ending when the green shoot emerges.

Pollination

The transfer of pollen from the male part of a flower to the female part, enabling fertilization and the production of seeds.

Photosynthesis

The process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize nutrients from carbon dioxide and water, producing oxygen as a byproduct.

Fertilizer

A substance added to soil to provide nutrients that promote plant growth and productivity.

Herbicide

A chemical substance used to control or destroy unwanted plants, commonly known as weeds.

Pesticide

A substance used for destroying insects or other organisms harmful to cultivated plants.
